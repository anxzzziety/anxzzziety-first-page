Do

1. Get to know some people or find reputable travel agencies before you go. They can help with navigation, cultural, and translation issues.

2. Inform your Credit Card company that you will be in Thailand/traveling. You do not want any annoying blocks on your account while trying to use it.

3. Visit the many temples (Wat).

4. Visit the many islands (Kho). Be prepared for some that are closer to larger tourist cities (Phuket, Pattaya), to be very crowded.

5. Either have a tolerance for or build up a tolerance for spicy foods. You may not be able to prevent the heat.

6. a. Get yourself used to the idea of using a bidet. You will see the hose form in some of your better hotels or buildings.

6. b. Or the rainbarrel and cup version, more noticeable as you travel away from touristy spots.

6. c. Pack hand sanitizer/wipes and even disposable gloves. They can assist in self-cleaning when the spicy foods strike.

7. Ask taxi/Tuk Tuk drivers to use their meter. This can prevent over charging scams.

8. Learn the BTS/MRT train system if you stay in Bangkok. It is a cheap method of getting around the city.

9. Learn to sit cross legged or with your legs under and feet directed behind you. This helps during a "Do Not" category.

10. Verify or declare any prescription medications that you plan on taking. Prevent legal issues.

11. Wear long pants and either short sleeve or long sleeve tops when visiting temples (Wat).

12. a. You will be required to remove your shoes and stage them outside of temples (Wat).

12. b. If you carry a backpack/small pack, have an empty bag to keep your shoes in, to prevent staging your shoes.

Do Not!!!

1. Criticize the Royal Family in any way. Safer to not even talk about them.

2. (Recently learned via the above mentioned "Thaiger" publication) Go without underwear while in public. I was not aware it was an issue, but now you know.

3. a.Wear shoes in homes or Temples(Wat).

3. b. Expose the bottom of your feet toward images of Buddha if you are sitting in a Wat. (above mentioned "D0" #9).

4. Use controlled substances. Marijuana is a big nope, even if legal where you are from.

5. Safer to not criticize the local/national government figures or be near protest demonstrations. Prevent being mistaken as a protester.

6. Wear shorts or tank top/spaghetti strap/see through shirts when visiting temples (Wat).
