The Good

1. Bangkok has a strong presence of the English language in signage and their skytrain system, which helps in navigation.

2. There is a good currency transfer rate from U.S. Dollar to Thai Baht.

3. There are numerous Temples (Thai translation is Wat) to visit. Many of which are very large and have elaborate artistic detail work inside and outside of the structures/statues.

4. Thailand has multiple Islands (Thai translation is Koh) all along the southern borders. Many of which have very clear water along their coastlines.

The Bad

1. There are many scams against tourist operated by taxi drivers. When using a cab, ask for meter use before getting in. If they say anything related to "no meter", do not get in.

2. Like many places outside of the U.S. borders, do not drink the water. Drink bottled water from sealed containers. Even ice can be an issue depending on the sources.

3. You must, and I repeat must, be able to handle spicy foods. Build up a tolerance before traveling.

4. Going beyond larger tourist cities and venturing into the rural areas, you will be traveling outside of the English language zones. A translator will be a necessity.

5. Be careful and study some cultural norms before traveling. Some liberties that we are used to, are shunned and can even be illegal there.
